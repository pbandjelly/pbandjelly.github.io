Dragging my feet

May 21, 2018

I graduate from the Naval Academy in 4 days. It's currently Commissioning Week, a week dedicated to honoring the graduating class. My family comes in on Wednesday to take part in some of the festivities. Lots of milestones are materializing. Graduation, commissioning, moving into my own apartment... I should be more excited than I currently am. But I'm not.

Maybe I'm burned out. That's possible, given that I have been in school for a long time. Or maybe my introverted voice is telling me to get away from people. That's also possible, given that an uncomfortable level of human interaction was necessary to maintain any sense of respect at school. Definitely had to step outside the comfort zone with that one. The exact reason is probably a combination of multiple factors... not only am I not excited, I also feel bored. And despondent. It's almost as if I am dragging my feet in this dirt road of life. I need something to happen.

The temporary solace I turn to when feeling extra blue is research. Yeah, I'm a nerd. Reading about new deep learning methods for neural networks. The "AI" industry is getting pretty saturated with the same neural network parameters being used for the same classification that nothing novel is getting done. Reading about top-end research on sites like arXiv gives me the psychedelic jump I need to get through one more day.

I didn't expect my first blog post to be so demoralizing. Hopefully the sentiment trend is upwards from here.
